How we use your personal data - privacy notice

The data controller is the Environment Agency. A data controller determines how and why personal data is processed.

The data we need

This data includes your name, contact details and any records of water use.

Why we need it

We collect the data to:

  • process your water use records
  • keep public registers up to date
  • contact you about your licence
  • register you to use our online water resources service
  • process your licence applications
  • amend or renew your existing licence
  • advertise your application for a new licence, if required

We need to do this to manage water resources in England. This is in the public interest and the data controller has official authority vested in it to do this.

Sharing and transferring personal data

We will not share your data with anyone outside of the Environment Agency without your consent unless we are allowed to do so by law. But we may pass your data on to our agents or representatives to process it for us.

We will not transfer your data outside the European Economic Area.

How long we hold personal data

We will keep your personal data required to access the Manage your water abstraction or impoundment licence service and for as long as you require access to the service.

We will keep other personal data for 7 years once the abstraction or impoundment licence ceases to have effect. After 7 years we will review retention in line with our standard information retention policy.

Automated decision-making and profiling

We will not use the information you provide:

  • to make a decision by automated means without any human involvement
  • for automated processing of personal data to check certain things about someone
  • For general information about how we treat your personal data, see our Personal Information Charter: www.gov.uk/government/
    organisations/environmentagency/
    about/personalinformationcharter
